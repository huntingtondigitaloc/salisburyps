COVID-19 Update

Winter Surge On Its Way!

Russian Icon Museum in Clinton, MA last week – Artistic representation of Mary Magdalene with lower eyelid bags.

On one hand, things seemed to be returning to normal. After weeks of declining numbers of COVID cases and deaths, however, the overall curve is beginning to show an uptick in cases – the start of our winter surge. An even closer look across the country reveals that the number of active COVID cases is still steady in the highly vaccinated states (or in the larger states, in highly vaccinated areas like Orange and Los Angeles Counties in CA) while starting to rise in states (or in parts of states such as the Inland Empire and Fresno County in CA) where vaccinations have not been as welcome. There has been an uptick in coronavirus cases throughout New England in the last two weeks as well. The number of coronavirus infections are also soaring in Europe. Coronavirus cases in Germany are surging to record highs. About 67% of Germans have been vaccinated but experts have warned that this number is not high enough to avoid future surges. And the number of the US population which has been fully vaccinated stands at a scary 58%. Canada is doing better and stands at 74% fully vaccinated.

Science clearly shows that vaccination decreases one’s chance of contracting a COVID infection or dying by a factor of 20 fold. This issue has become politicized to the point that the unvaccinated are risking their lives and the lives of family members. Most of the resisters just consider this an overreach of government into their personal lives and know or care very little about the science of the pandemic. We are sad to see this divisiveness in our country and other parts of the world which are considered to be more advanced in many ways. The number of cases per 100,000 inhabitants is a metric used by scientists to measure the severity of the pandemic and to compare the spread of the disease in various parts of the world. Unfortunately, the US, Canada and Europe, areas where vaccines are readily available but vaccination resistance is high, top this list, whereas areas of the world where vaccines are in short supply but people conform to masking and social distancing rules, such as Latin American, Africa, the Middle East and the Asia-Pacific regions, have far lower numbers of infections per 100,000 population. Mandatory vaccination rules might help but have been challenged in the courts. Perhaps Singapore, a country that is more than 80% vaccinated, has a good suggestion for the rest of the world – Singapore has stopped covering costs to treat COVID patients who refused to be vaccinated.


ON THE PLASTIC SURGERY FRONT:

This past week saw both of us working out of the Salisbury Plastic Surgery office in Massachusetts. We visited the Russian Icon Museum in Clinton MA and were very impressed by the extensive collection housed there. Yes, we found a painting of Mary Magdalene with pretty severe lower lid bags and thought of the many patients we see with this problem in our practices every week. The lower eyelid bulge that some of us get is caused by an excess of fat, 100 percent hereditary in nature (you cannot get lower eyelid bags if your father or mother did not have them). This fat is the only fat in the body that is not under dietary control – the bags do not change in size if you gain or if you lose weight. These bags can show up in patients in their teens or twenties. The only way to remove these bags is by surgical fat removal. This fat can be removed by one of two approaches – in younger patients without skin excess, the fat can be removed without a visible scar, from the inside of the eyelid, but if it is desirable to remove excess skin, an external incision will be designed under the eyelashes. Once removed, the excess fat will not return. Call us if you would like to discuss your concerns and we will be happy to go over all of your options with you.
