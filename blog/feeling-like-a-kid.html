Feeling Like A Child Again!

Light show at the Tower Hill Botanic Garden in Boylston MA

The holiday season is upon us. In Orange County, at this time of year, we enjoy the Newport Beach Boat Parade and in Disneyland the Christmas Fantasy Parade – we will be taking some of our grandchildren to the Disney Parade next week. Last week we were fortunate to enjoy the equivalent of these events in Massachusetts and were able to visit the Tower Hill Botanic Garden which the Worcester Horticulture Society decorates with thousands of lights. Albeit, a little chillier than wandering around Newport Beach or Disneyland at this time of year, but wandering around the gardens and letting the holiday spirit soak into us was very satisfying – such events bring the “kid” out in all of us and bring back memories of Christmases in Canada or Minnesota while we were growing up.

The COVID front brings us some good news and some less favorable news this week. The early Omicron variant reports are somewhat optimistic. Yes, the new variant might be even more contagious (spreads more easily) than prior variants, but the symptoms which infected patients experience are not as severe. The vast majority of COVID hospitalizations and deaths in this country remain due to the Delta variant. Two years into this pandemic, 780,000 Americans have lost their lives to the COVID virus, and coronavirus deaths are currently running at about 860 per day. The good news regarding the vaccines is that they protect us from this new variant as well, but the bad news is that protection from the vaccines wanes more rapidly than we were led to believe initially, making boosters necessary, and we are fairly certain, continued boosters in the future. People who have been fully vaccinated continue to get ill with the COVID virus but usually with relatively mild symptoms – in Massachusetts, 1.8% of fully vaccinated people have had a break though COVID infection. Unfortunately, the number of coronavirus cases are rising across the nation, perhaps related to Thanksgiving, Hanukkah and Christmas - officials are predicting another winter surge. But of course, areas where more people are more likely to be vaccinated are showing less COVID infections that areas with lower vaccine acceptance. What can we do to limit the spread of infections over the holiday season? Take care of yourself first and make sure you are vaccinated and boosted. And if meeting with friends or relatives over the holidays, participants should get a negative COVID test (labs providing free tests and home kits are readily available) prior to attending large gatherings in order to minimize the chance of infection.

As mentioned in our last blog, this is a time of year when many folks have a “slow time” business wise and like using this time to take care of their aesthetic needs. Our schedules are pretty full into the New Year but we would like to share a patient who came in for a follow up recently, six years after her facial rejuvenation surgery. You can see that her jaw line and eye area look much better than they did previously but in a very natural way and she does not have “the done look”. For every type of procedure we do, we do encounter patients who request being “overdone” but the vast majority express the opposite desire – they wish to look better without looking done! When discussing facial rejuvenation with patients, we stress that we will pull the sagging underlying muscles into their original position, but that we do not pull the skin tight, so as to avoid the horizontal pleats and other signs of overly aggressive rejuvenation. Please call for an appointment, if you would like to achieve this kind of result for yourself and be ready to face the new year with renewed confidence.
