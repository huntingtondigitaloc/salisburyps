What is BodyTite™?

If dieting and exercise are not giving you the results you’re after, you may want to consider BodyTite™, a 3-dimensional skin tightening procedure that can be combined with fat removal. Board-certified plastic surgeon Dr. Deborah K. Ekstrom at Salisbury Plastic Surgery in Worcester offers BodyTite™ to residents of Worcester, Shrewsbury, and nearby communities in Massachusetts.

BodyTite™ is a device that uses radiofrequency assisted liposuction (RFAL™) technology to tighten the skin and may be combined with fat removal. The procedure is performed in our comfortable and relaxing office. You can treat the neck, back, bra area, inner and outer thighs, inner knees, armpits, and abdomen with BodyTite™. During clinical studies BodyTite™ showed up to a 40 percent skin contraction, making this procedure a popular alternative to invasive liposuction surgery.

How Does BodyTite™ Work?

The BodyTite™ device has two components: a cannula and an electrode. The electrode glides on top of the skin, and the cannula is inserted under the skin through a small incision. A radiofrequency current flows from the internal cannula to the external electrode. The temperature is regulated in real time throughout the procedure to ensure safety, and the fat is liquefied and removed from the body through a tube on the other end of the device. The procedure requires local anesthesia or light sedation if you prefer.

Benefits of BodyTite™

Our patients find BodyTite™ offers the following benefits:

  • Single procedure
  • Local anesthesia
  • Small incision
  • Less post-op pain, bruising
  • Less invasive alternative to liposuction
  • Results that continue to improve over a six month period
  • Full recovery in just a few days
  • Scar-free procedure

What Kind of Results Can I Expect From a BodyTite™ Treatment?

Overall you can expect improved skin tightness and contouring in addition to a reduction of fat in the treatment area(s). In addition, our patients typically notice a reduction in cellulite type ripples. The results are long-lasting when coupled with a healthy diet and exercise routine.
